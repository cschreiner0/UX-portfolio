A website redesign that focuses on the resident artists and their vision as a non-profit organization.

Overview

Leading a team of six designers and web developers, the redesign encompassed the necessities and foundation of the company. The Laboratory Residency Spokane website redesign reflects their resident artists and their vision as a non-profit.

The Challange


To maintain the same content but highlight the artists and enhance the content to be usable for all audiences.


To understand what needed to be improved, we met with the client to understand their organization's needs and as a team, we evaluated their current website. I organized weekly team meetings to split the workload for optimized work efficiently and track weekly progress. The first task my teammate and I did was conduct user research through SWOT analysis (strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, threats) and contextual inquiry. Through this analysis, we were able to begin redesigning with specific goals.


Evaluation Goals

  • Easy to update – Residents and events are going in and out frequently.
  • Showcases Resident Work – From the past, and present throughout the entire website with captions.
  • Identify a Visual Identity – Professional for visitors, but inspirational and artist centered.
  • Easy to Navigate – All sections are easy to find and have meaning. Content of each is understandable relatively quickly before going into depth.
  • Information sorted well for different audiences – Administration, resident artists, collaborators, curators, donators, community. Draws all of them in.
  • Organized – Past residents and their history, current residents and their info, events, staff, etc.

The Solution


I conducted user and organizational research, created the usability test, earned IRB approval, recruited participants, administered usability testing, and analyzed data which quickly turned into actionable recommendations to stakeholders and designers.

Wireframes and Reorganization: Content layout revision and redistribution.

What are wireframes? The basic outline of the content and layout design which is easy to create experiment with and loosely create the layout of the final product.



Prototyping and Coding: Creating the final product and making it real.


We combined the art direction and wireframing to create a working prototype. I sent to prototype to the client for feedback and we revised it to meet their needs while keeping the constraints of the users' needs. Currently we are in the process of coding the basic website and then work with web developers to complete the website.



Final Takeaways

Working with UX designers, web developers, and Laboratory Residency Spokane was a great experience as it brought me further down the path to designing and coding for websites. Leading weekly meetings and regularly contacting the client with questions and updates on our progress was a learning experience that was a little nerve wracking but also confidence inducing. I also learned how to be a UX designer and researcher that works on all sections of the process, including working with web developers which was a newer experience for me. I thoroughly enjoyed working with Laboratory and my teammates and expect to further build upon these skills with future teammates and clientele.
