How-To Bamboo App

A WikiHow App Redesign that has enhanced accessibility, navigability, and usability

Overview

How-To Bamboo's user interface is clean and easy to navigate with accessibility features of languages and read-aloud audio included. Navigating through long articles has never been easier with table of contents and return to the top features included. This simple but fluid how-to app has increased the usability for many diverse and general audiences.

The Challange


To maintain the same content but enhance the usability and design of the app.


To understand what needed to be improved, I analyzed the WikiHow app and website for strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and threats (SWOT analysis). Through this analysis, I was able to begin redesigning with specific goals.


Evaluation Goals

  • Information and navigation needs to be reorganized and restructured.
  • The search bar should be easier to find.
  • The app supports WikiHow's diverse audience through accessbile design: language, privacy, and display options.

The Solution


To determine which re-design iteration works best for users, I created two design protoypes, known as A/B protoypes. A/B testing has not been conducted yet but I am excited to continue learning about this methodology and do the study in the future.


Final Takeaways

This was a great challenge as it brought me on the path to designing for mobile apps, so quite a few lessons were learned. I began involving diverse audiences and accessible features in my designs which has been a journey of learning about their diverse needs. I also began understanding the constraints of creating an app and the layout, which both need thoughtful consideration for all users in order for a great experience to be had.
